2018 Social Impact Hackathon

180 Degrees Consulting is proud to host our first-ever social impact hackathon.
Solve complex organisational problems for our client, Out For Australia,
meet talented students like yourself and make tangible social impact on October 20-21.

  • Find out more

Introduction

Be part of a weekend of ideas, innovation and impact by participating in 180 Degrees Consulting’s inaugural Social Impact Hackathon.

This Social Impact Hackathon will be held in conjunction with Out For Australia, which is a volunteer-run non-for-profit that seeks to support and mentor aspiring LGBTIQ+ students and professionals navigate their way through the early stages of their career.

Despite marriage equality and increasing push for Safe Schools, research shows that only 32% of LGBTIQ+ people are out to everyone with whom they work, and this is even lower for young LGBTIQ+ people. Over the course of a weekend, you will help Out For Australia solve a strategic issue and be part of shaping a safer and more diverse and inclusive workplace in teams of 4-5.

The event will bring together talented and innovative students, industry mentors and representatives from Out for Australia to co-design a strategy to empower inclusion and sense of community among LGBTIQ+ professionals and students. We invite anyone with bright ideas, appetite for problem-solving and a genuine desire to make a social difference to register today!

On the Day

In the lead up to the Hackathon, we will be releasing the briefing pack to participants on October 17. Included in this briefing pack:

  • The problem brief (which will also outline key deliverables)
  • Resources to help get you started, including background data, potential areas to research, problem solving frameworks
  • Marking criteria you will be judged against
  • Logistical information, including run sheets, what to bring

The Social Impact Hackathon will be hosted at the Nous Group offices at the following address:

L34/60 Margaret Street
Sydney NSW 2000

Throughout the Hackathon, you will be able to come in and out of the offices when necessary. Each team will have their own area in Nous’ coworking space. Mentors will communicate with their allocated teams and will be able to provide guidance electronically and in person. There will be a catered lunch.

At the end of the second day, each team will have the opportunity to present their strategy to a panel of judges. Prizes will be awarded to the winning teams. A more detailed itinerary of the Hackathon is as follows:

Who can attend?
All students from the University of Sydney are invited to attend. All current 180 Degrees Consulting consultants from the University of New South Wales and Macquarie University can also attend.

Since this is our first time running the Hackathon, we want to pilot it successfully before rolling registrations out to universities Sydney-wide in future Hackathons.

Do I need to know how to code?
Nope! The Hackathon is focused on strategy rather than product. If you have technical skills and would like to use them to create a prototype, then feel free! However, not coding a solution will not disadvantage you.

What if I don’t have a team?
You can still sign up here and we will do our best to put you into a team. Your application will not be disadvantaged if you are applying as an individual!

Will there be food?
Lunch will be provided for both days, but feel free to bring your own snacks so that you refuel throughout the event!

What if I don't have experience in social impact work?
You don't have to be already saving the world to start making a difference! We will have mentors and Out For Australia representatives there on the day to guide you through whatever questions you may have.

I know 180 Degrees Consulting is mainly a consultancy service. What if I haven't consulted before in the past?
Prior consulting knowledge is not necessary. We strongly encourage anyone interested to attend our briefing event on Monday October 15 to find out more about the Hackathon and to attend some sponsor-led workshops that should equip you with some tools that will help you through October 20-21. We will also be providing resources before and on the day of the Hacakthon to make sure students of all skillsets and experience levels are catered for. Our mentors will also guide you through any methodologies you may find confusing. You will not be disadvantaged if you don't have prior consulting experience!.

I still have another question, who can I talk to?
Still not sure what to expect? Wondering whether you have the right knowledge or skills? Have all your questions answered at our information session before the event on 15 October. Register your interest in the Hackathon and we will give you more details closer to the date.

We will run through the event and provide you with some tips and training to prepare you for the Hackathon, and you will have the opportunity to hear from people who have participated in other Hackathons in the past.

Out For Australia

Out for Australia is a not-for-profit that supports and mentors aspiring LGBTIQ+ professionals as they navigate their way through the early stages of their career. It provides visible role models, mentors, content and targeted support to aspiring LGBTIQ+ professionals, striving to create an Australia where every LGBTIQ+ student and aspiring professional is confident to be their authentic self in workplaces which celebrate diversity.

On the day, you will work closely with Emily Scott, the CEO of Out for Australia, to address the problem faced by her organisation.
