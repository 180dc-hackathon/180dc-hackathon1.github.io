2018 Social Impact Hackathon

180 Degrees Consulting is proud to host our first-ever social impact hackathon.
Solve complex organisational problems for our client, Out For Australia,
meet talented students like yourself and make tangible social impact on October 20-21.

  • Find out more

Introduction

Be part of a weekend of ideas, innovation and impact by participating in 180 Degrees Consulting’s inaugural Social Impact Hackathon.

This Social Impact Hackathon will address inclusion of LGBTIQ+ students and professionals. Despite marriage equality and advances in LGBTIQ+ rights across Australia, research shows that only 32% of LGBTIQ+ people are out to everyone with whom they work, and this is even lower for young LGBT+ people.

The event will bring together talented and innovative students, industry mentors and representatives from Out for Australia, our not-for-profit client, to co-design a strategy for better strengthening the sense of community among LGBTIQ+ professionals and students.

Launch: October 15

We will kick off the Hackathon with a 2 hour information session where we will talk through the challenge brief and help build your understanding of Out for Australia. Kick start the hackathon with a 2 hour session where you will hear from our Community Partners as they talk through the challenge statements. This would be the best opportunity for you to understand the challenge statements and the profile of the beneficiaries.

Hack: October 20 - 21

Join us at Nous Group Offices to ideate and even prototype your solution. There will be a variety of workshops and mentors to guide you as you develop your solution and strategy for the final judging round on 21 October 2018.

On the Day

The Social Impact Hackathon will be hosted at the Nous Group offices at the following address:

L34/60 Margaret Street
Sydney NSW 2000

Please arrive at the lobby by 10AM and representatives from 180 Degrees Consulting will be there to receive you.

Throughout the day, you will be able to come in and out of the offices when necessary. Each team will have their own area in Nous’ coworking space. Mentors will communicate with their allocated teams and will be able to provide guidance electronically and in person. There will be a catered lunch.

At the end of the second day, each team will have the opportunity to present their strategy to a panel of judges. Prizes will be awarded to the winning teams.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Who can attend?
All students from the University of Sydney are invited to attend, and so are all current 180 Degrees Consulting consultants from the University of New South Wales and Macquarie University.

2. Do I need to know how to code?
Nope! The Hackathon is focused on strategy rather than product. If you have technical skills and would like to use them to create a prototype, then feel free! However, not coding a solution will not disadvantage you.

3. What if I don’t have a team?
You can sign up here and we will do our best to put you into a team.

4. Will there be food?
Lunch will be provided for both days, but feel free to bring your own snacks so that you refuel throughout the event!

5. I still have another question, who can I talk to?
Still not sure what to expect? Wondering whether you have the right knowledge or skills? Have all your questions answered at our information session before the event on 15 October. Register your interest in the Hackathon and we will give you more details closer to the date.

We will run through the event and provide you with some tips and training to prepare you for the Hackathon, and you will have the opportunity to hear from people who have participated in other Hackathons in the past.

Out For Australia

Out for Australia is a not-for-profit that supports and mentors aspiring LGBTIQ professionals as they navigate their way through the early stages of their career. It provides visible role models, mentors, content and targeted support to aspiring LGBTIQ professionals, striving to create an Australia where every LGBTIQ student and aspiring professional is confident to be their authentic self in workplaces which celebrate diversity.

On the day, you will work closely with Emily Scott, the CEO of Out for Australia, to address the problem faced by her organisation.
